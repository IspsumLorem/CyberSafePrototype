Keeping Yourself Safe: The Basics!

7th Septemeber 2022

The last thing you want online is for a stranger to know too much about you. It may be tempting to put everything about yourself online, but for every friend that'll see it, there will also be people who you wouldn't want seeing it.

The thing about the internet is that once it's online, it's there for everyone to see. Even 'deleting' something you posted doesn't necessarily mean it's gone. People have the power to save that information for themselves.

One thing you should always keep in mind online is to never be specific as to where you live. Countries are generally ok, but stating your city makes it easier for people to figure out who you really are

Being anonymous online is something everyone deserves. Making sure that you aren't specific on where you're from makes it harder for people to figure out where you're from and who you are.

But everyone else does it!

A recent trend online has been 'carrds', little about me pages that introduce social media users to the person behind the account they just clicked on.

However, these carrds tend to contain more information than necessary! While it's a good place to list your interests and to connect with other users, it's not a good place to list:

  • Age
  • Gender/Sexual Identity
  • Race
  • Personal Traumas (this is becoming quite frequent!)

These things don't have to be publically stated and it's very common for these things to be used against you. The veil of anonimity makes people much crueler online and can cause you to feel unsafe because of it. The internet should be a place about connecting with others, not a place to feel hurt

All in all, the internet is about expressing yourself and you can do whatever you want! However, you should be careful about what you post online and keep your personal information private. A safe user is a step towards a safer world wide web
